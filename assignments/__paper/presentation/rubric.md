# In-Class Research Presentation Rubric (5 Points Total)

## 1. Understanding of Research & Argument (0–1 point)
- **1** = Clearly explains the research question, theoretical idea, and what they’re testing (in their own words)  
- **0.5** = Some understanding but unclear or partially incorrect  
- **0** = Cannot clearly explain the project  

---

## 2. Interpretation of Evidence (0–1 point)
- **1** = Clearly explains results and connects them back to hypotheses  
- **0.5** = Mentions results but interpretation is weak or unclear  
- **0** = No real interpretation of findings  

---

## 3. Integration of Methods & Data (0–1 point)
- **1** = Demonstrates basic understanding of data, variables, and method (correct at a high level)  
- **0.5** = Mentions these but superficially or with confusion  
- **0** = No meaningful discussion of analysis  

---

## 4. Organization & Preparation (0–1 point)
- **1** = Clear structure, smooth flow, focused; presentation feels practiced  
- **0.5** = Some structure but uneven or slightly rushed/disjointed  
- **0** = Disorganized or clearly unprepared  

---

## 5. Delivery & Communication (0–1 point)
- **1** = Clear, audible, reasonably engaging; not overly reliant on reading slides  
- **0.5** = Understandable but flat, rushed, or heavily read  
- **0** = Difficult to follow or disengaged  

---

## Quick Scoring Heuristic (Optional)

- Do they understand their argument? (0–1)  
- Do they understand their results? (0–1)  
- Do they understand what they did? (0–1)  
- Was it organized/prepared? (0–1)  
- Could I follow them easily? (0–1)
